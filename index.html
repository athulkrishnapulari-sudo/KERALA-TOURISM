Explore Kerala

GOD'S OWN COUNTRY

THE SOUL OF KERALA

ALLEPEY

Alleppey is known for its beautiful backwaters and iconic houseboats. On your visit, you can stay overnight on a floating boat, and watch the sunset at Alappuzha Beach.

KOVALAM

Kovalam is a beautiful coastal destination known for its crescent-shaped beaches and the iconic red-and-white Vizhinjam Lighthouse.

MUNNAR

Munnar is a beautiful hill station known for its misty valleys and endless green tea plantations.On your visit, you can stroll through the lush tea estates and hear your voice echo at the scenic Echo Point.

WAYANAD

A weekend getaway to Wayanad is a perfect escape into Kerala’s lush green paradise. Start your journey at the fascinating Edakkal Caves, home to ancient rock carvings that whisper tales of the past.

FORT KOCHI

A historic coastal neighborhood blending Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences, renowned for its colonial architecture, cultural landmarks, and iconic Chinese fishing nets along the Arabian Sea.

THEKKADY

Thekkady is a dream destination for adventure travellers and Nature lovers alike. The region is also home to the richly bio-diverse Periyar Tiger Reserve.

Traditions of
Kerala

Kerala is a land where ancient customs are still part of daily life. From the high-energy festivals on the water to the peaceful rituals of healing, every tradition tells a story of our heritage

Kadhakali

Kerala’s world-famous dance-drama where actors use vibrant makeup and ornate costumes to tell ancient legends. Without speaking a single word, they communicate epic stories through "Mudras" (hand gestures) and intense facial expressions.

Vallam Kali

Vallam Kali is Kerala's legendary "Snake Boat Race" and the world's largest team sport. Over 100 rowers cram into a single 100-foot wooden boat, slicing through the backwaters in perfect, explosive rhythm.

Kalari Payattu

Kalaripayattu (or Kalari) is an ancient Indian martial art from Kerala, considered the "mother of all martial arts," featuring dynamic movements, animal postures, yoga-like stretches, and weapon training (sticks, swords, spears).
